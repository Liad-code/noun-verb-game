## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### The Overwhelmed Planner (Sarah)
**Context:** 
Sarah is a 32-year-old marketing manager who plans 3-4 trips per year for herself and her partner. She typically spends 4-6 hours per trip scouring multiple platforms and comparing options.

**Problem Experience:** 
She manages her travel planning by opening Kayak, Google Flights, Booking.com, and TripAdvisor simultaneously. The process requires her to constantly re-enter dates and preferences on each platform. She frequently experiences decision fatigue from the overwhelming number of options, leading her to second-guess decisions or experience buyer's remorse when she spots a better deal post-booking.

**Success Vision:** 
Sarah simply tells Tripo, "Me and my partner, Barcelona, last week of June, €1500 budget, we like boutique hotels and rooftop bars." Tripo immediately curates a shortlist of flights and hotels matching these precise constraints, complete with direct affiliate booking links. The entire process takes 5 minutes instead of 5 hours.

### Secondary Users

#### The Family Trip Coordinator (David)
**Context:**
David is a 41-year-old father of two. He plans 1-2 major family trips per year and must navigate complex, non-negotiable constraints like toddler-friendly accommodations, luggage logistics, and a strong preference against connecting flights.

**Problem Experience:**
He struggles to filter for family rooms, direct flights, and kid-friendly activities because each platform handles these filters differently and inconsistently. Finding options that satisfy all criteria simultaneously is a major point of friction.

**Success Vision:**
Tripo remembers his permanent constraints: "2 adults, 2 kids under 6, need direct flights only." It automatically applies this context across every single suggestion without David needing to re-enter or explicitly ask for these filters in subsequent searches.

### User Journey

1. **Discovery & Onboarding:** The user signs up for an account to save their trips. They are immediately greeted by the conversational AI, which explains it can handle entire trip bookings.
2. **Initial Request:** The user provides a high-level prompt combining destination, dates, budget, and specific preferences (e.g., "Family trip to Tokyo next fall, direct flights, close to transit").
3. **Conversational Refinement (Core Usage):** The AI engages in a back-and-forth dialogue to further narrow down options, confirming constraints without ever asking for previously provided information.
4. **Curated Output & Booking (Success Moment):** The AI presents a unified list of flight and hotel options sourced live from integrated APIs (Kayak, Booking.com, etc.). Each matches the exact context provided. The user clicks the direct booking links provided by the AI to secure their trip.
5. **Long-term Value:** Because Tripo requires a user account, the platform saves their past trips and permanent preferences, making subsequent trip planning completely frictionless.
